Draft Digital Competition Act, 2024 (Mock Detailed Legal Structure)
An Act
to provide for an ex-ante regulatory mechanism for Systemically Significant Digital Enterprises operating in certain Core Digital Services, to ensure fair and contestable digital markets in India, and for matters connected therewith or incidental thereto.
BE it enacted by Parliament in the Seventy-fifth Year of the Republic of India as follows:
CHAPTER I: PRELIMINARY
Section 1. Short title, extent, and commencement
1.1. This Act may be called the Digital Competition Act, 2024. 1.2. It extends to the whole of India and shall apply to all enterprises, howsoever incorporated or domiciled, provided they meet the criteria for designation as a Systemically Significant Digital Enterprise (SSDE) under Chapter II, in respect of their operations within the territory of the Republic of India. 1.3. Commencement: It shall come into force on such date as the Central Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, appoint, and different dates may be appointed for different provisions of this Act.
Section 2. Objectives and Interpretation
2.1. Primary Objective: The primary objective of this Act is to proactively regulate the conduct of large digital enterprises to prevent the creation of competition barriers and to ensure fair, equitable, and non-discriminatory access to Core Digital Services. 2.2. Interpretation: The provisions of this Act shall be interpreted in a manner that is consistent with the principles of promoting and sustaining competition in digital markets.
Section 3. Definitions
In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires— 3.1. “Adjacency” means and includes the interlinking, cross-promotion, or functional interdependence of a Core Digital Service (CDS) with an ancillary, complementary, or vertically related service, product, application, or feature, howsoever denominated. 3.2. “Business User” means any individual, partnership, company, firm, association of persons, or any other entity, whether incorporated under any law or not, which uses the Core Digital Service of an SSDE for the purpose of offering, advertising, or facilitating the exchange of its own goods or services to End Users. 3.3. “Core Digital Service (CDS)” means any service, or category of services, determined and notified by the Central Government, and includes, but is not restricted to, the following categories: 3.3.1. Online search engine services, including all forms of information indexing and retrieval. 3.3.2. Online social networking services, including messaging and communication platforms. 3.3.3. Video sharing platform services and content hosting services. 3.3.4. Operating systems and device platforms, including software distribution frameworks. 3.3.5. Cloud computing services, including Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS) and Platform as a Service (PaaS). 3.3.6. Online advertising services, encompassing ad-networks, ad-exchanges, ad-servers, and programmatic advertising services. 3.4. “End User” means any natural or legal person, other than a Business User, who accesses, consumes, or utilizes a Core Digital Service, whether free of charge or against monetary consideration. 3.5. “Systemically Significant Digital Enterprise (SSDE)” means an enterprise designated by the Competition Commission of India (CCI) under Section 4, having a 'Significant Presence' in relation to a Core Digital Service in the Indian market, and includes its legal and operational subsidiaries. 3.6. “Digital Markets and Data Unit (DMDU)” means the specialized unit established under Section 16 within the CCI for the effective and timely administration and enforcement of the provisions of this Act. 3.7. “Non-Public Data” means any data, including aggregated or anonymized data, obtained by the SSDE through the usage of its Core Digital Service, which is not publicly accessible or readily available to third-party Business Users of the platform.
CHAPTER II: DESIGNATION OF SYSTEMICALLY SIGNIFICANT DIGITAL ENTERPRISES
Section 4. Criteria for Designation (The Twin Test and Qualitative Assessment)
4.1. An enterprise shall be deemed to have a "Significant Presence" in the relevant Core Digital Service in the Indian market if it fulfils the criteria specified in both sub-sections 4.2 (Significant Financial Strength) and 4.3 (Significant Spread). 4.2. Significant Financial Strength Test: The enterprise meets this test if it satisfies any one of the following thresholds in the preceding two financial years: 4.2.1. Global Turnover: Exceeding an amount to be specified in Schedule I; OR 4.2.2. India-Specific Turnover: Exceeding an amount to be specified in Schedule I; OR 4.2.3. Global Market Capitalization/Fair Market Value: Exceeding an amount to be specified in Schedule I. 4.2.4. The Central Government shall, by notification, amend the precise monetary thresholds specified in Schedule I. 4.3. Significant Spread Test: The enterprise meets this test if it satisfies both of the following user thresholds in the preceding financial year: 4.3.1. End Users: Exceeding a number to be specified in Schedule I of unique, active End Users in India accessing the Core Digital Service. 4.3.2. Business Users: Exceeding a number to be specified in Schedule I of unique, active Business Users in India relying on the Core Digital Service to reach End Users.
Section 5. Procedure for Designation
5.1. Initiation of Inquiry: The CCI shall, on its own motion (suo motu) or upon receipt of a reference from the Central Government or a complaint, initiate an inquiry to determine if an enterprise meets the criteria under Section 4. 5.2. Power to Call for Information: The CCI may, through the DMDU, require any enterprise, including its ultimate parent entity, subsidiaries, or associated persons, to furnish all financial, operational, and user data deemed necessary for the inquiry, within a stipulated time frame. 5.3. Suo Motu Designation (Qualitative): Notwithstanding the quantitative thresholds in Section 4.2 and 4.3, the CCI may, by recording specific reasons in writing, designate an enterprise as an SSDE if it is satisfied that the enterprise's strategic position, control over access to the market, pervasive network effects, and ability to leverage data confers equivalent, entrenched, or near-permanent market power. 5.4. Principles of Natural Justice: Before issuing a final designation order, the CCI shall provide the enterprise with a reasonable opportunity to be heard, including access to the investigation report (subject to confidentiality claims) and the right to file objections or representations. 5.5. Issuance and Notification: Upon satisfaction, the CCI shall issue a detailed order designating the enterprise as an SSDE, which shall be published in the Official Gazette and served upon the designated enterprise. 5.6. Validity and Review: The designation shall remain in force for a period of three (3) financial years from the date of the order, subject to review and renewal thereafter, upon a fresh determination by the CCI.
CHAPTER III: OBLIGATIONS OF SYSTEMICALLY SIGNIFICANT DIGITAL ENTERPRISES
Section 6. General Obligations and Compliance Mandate
6.1. Every enterprise designated as an SSDE shall, at all times, ensure strict and verifiable compliance with the mandatory obligations set forth in this Chapter in relation to the Core Digital Services for which it is designated. 6.2. The SSDE shall submit a periodic compliance report to the CCI, detailing the measures adopted to ensure adherence to each obligation, in such form and frequency as may be specified in the Regulations.
Section 7. Prohibition on Self-Preferencing and Discrimination
7.1. An SSDE shall not, directly or indirectly, through technical, legal, or commercial means, treat its own products or services, including those of its subsidiaries or affiliates, more favourably than the products or services offered by its Business Users or other third parties using the SSDE’s Core Digital Service. 7.2. The prohibition includes, but is not limited to: 7.2.1. Ranking and Indexing: Granting preferential, automatic, or disproportionately higher ranking, indexing, or display prominence to the SSDE’s products or services in search results, recommended lists, or navigational interfaces, without a legitimate and transparent technical justification that is equally applied to third parties. 7.2.2. Pre-installation and Default Settings: Pre-installing or requiring the pre-installation of the SSDE’s applications or services on devices, operating systems, or interfaces, or making them the default setting, without providing an equivalent, easy, and immediate option for uninstallation, change, or side-loading of competing alternatives.
Section 8. Restriction on Anti-Steering and Transaction Obstruction
8.1. An SSDE shall not restrict, prevent, or disincentivise Business Users from communicating, promoting, or transacting directly with their End Users, whether on the SSDE's platform or outside of it. 8.2. Transparent Fee Structures: Any fee structure or commission imposed by the SSDE on transactions facilitated through its Core Digital Service must be transparent, proportionate to the service rendered, and non-discriminatory towards Business Users. 8.3. Payment Systems: The SSDE shall not mandate the exclusive use of its own payment processing system for transactions between Business Users and End Users, and shall permit the use of third-party payment systems subject only to reasonable, proportionate, and verifiable security and fraud prevention requirements.
Section 9. Data Usage and Combination Restrictions
9.1. Restriction on Competitive Use: An SSDE shall not use Non-Public Data, howsoever defined, generated from the activities of its Business Users on its Core Digital Service, to offer services, products, or features that compete with those Business Users. 9.2. Data Combination Consent: The SSDE shall be prohibited from combining, cross-using, or aggregating personal data sourced from different Core Digital Services or from services provided by third parties, unless it has obtained a clear, explicit, and separate affirmative consent from the End User for each specific combination and purpose. 9.3. Data Portability and Access: The SSDE shall provide, free of charge and in real-time where feasible, effective tools and mechanisms to allow both End Users and Business Users to port, transfer, or access data generated through their use of the CDS to competing platforms or service providers in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format.
Section 10. Fair and Non-Discriminatory Access and Interoperability
10.1. Access Conditions: An SSDE, particularly one operating an App Store, Operating System, or distribution framework, shall ensure that the terms, conditions, application programming interfaces (APIs), and pricing of access for Business Users (developers) are Fair, Reasonable, and Non-Discriminatory (FRAND). 10.2. Mandatory Interoperability: An SSDE shall be obligated to provide, upon reasonable request and free of charge, the necessary technical interfaces and data formats to enable effective interoperability and portability with the SSDE's Core Digital Services for third-party service providers, where such interoperability is technically feasible and does not compromise national security or genuine public safety concerns.
Section 11. Transparency and Auditing Requirements
11.1. Performance Data Access: An SSDE shall provide Business Users with transparent, continuous, and reasonable access to the data on their own performance, visibility, clicks, impressions, and sales on the SSDE’s platform, as necessary for those users to compete effectively. 11.2. Auditing Rights: The CCI, or an independent third-party auditor appointed by the CCI, shall have the right to conduct audits, at the SSDE’s expense, of the SSDE's internal systems, data practices, and technical compliance mechanisms to verify adherence to the obligations under this Chapter.
CHAPTER IV: ENFORCEMENT AND PROCEDURE FOR INQUIRY
Section 12. Inquiry into Non-Compliance
12.1. The CCI, acting through the DMDU, may initiate an inquiry if it has sufficient grounds, prima facie, to believe that an SSDE has contravened or is likely to contravene any of the mandatory obligations under Chapter III. 12.2. Information Gathering: The CCI shall have the powers vested in a Civil Court under the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, including the power to summon and enforce the attendance of any person, examine him on oath, and compel the production of documents. 12.3. Inquiry Report: The DMDU shall prepare a detailed inquiry report summarizing the evidence of non-compliance, which shall be placed before the CCI for final determination.
Section 13. Power of the Commission to Pass Orders
13.1. Where the CCI, after considering the inquiry report and the response of the SSDE, finds that a contravention of Chapter III has occurred, it may pass any or all of the following orders: 13.1.1. Directing the SSDE to cease and desist from such non-compliant conduct immediately and take such steps as may be specified in the order. 13.1.2. Directing the SSDE to modify its technical or commercial practices within a specified time frame to ensure full compliance with the relevant obligation. 13.1.3. Imposing such financial penalties as provided for in Chapter V. 13.2. Interim Directions: The CCI shall have the power to pass interim directions to prevent immediate, irreversible, or irreparable harm to competition, pending the final inquiry and determination. 13.3. Structural Remedies (Recommendation): In cases of persistent, repeated, or egregious non-compliance where behavioral remedies have proven insufficient, the CCI may, after consulting with the Central Government, recommend the imposition of structural remedies, including the divestment or separation of a part of the SSDE’s business or assets.
CHAPTER V: OFFENCES AND PENALTIES
Section 14. Penalty for Non-Compliance with Obligations
14.1. An SSDE that fails to comply with any of the mandatory obligations specified in Chapter III of this Act shall be liable to a penalty which shall not exceed ten per cent (10%) of the global turnover of the SSDE or the entire group to which it belongs, calculated for the preceding financial year. 14.2. Criteria for Determining Penalty: While determining the quantum of penalty under Section 14.1, the CCI shall have due regard to the following factors: 14.2.1. The nature, gravity, and duration of the contravention. 14.2.2. The mitigating or aggravating factors. 14.2.3. The actual or potential harm caused to competition and consumers in the Indian market. 14.2.4. The profit derived by the SSDE from the contravention.
Section 15. Penalty for Non-Compliance with Directions of the Commission
15.1. If an SSDE fails to comply with any direction or order issued by the CCI under Section 13, it shall be liable to a penalty not exceeding ten lakh rupees (₹10,00,000) for each day during which such non-compliance continues, subject to a maximum of twenty crore rupees (₹20,00,00,000).
Section 16. Penalty for Making False Statements or Furnishing False Information
16.1. If any person fails to comply with a lawful requisition of the CCI or furnishes any information or makes any statement that is false or misleading in any material respect during an inquiry under this Act, the CCI may impose a penalty not exceeding ten crore rupees (₹10,00,00,000).
CHAPTER VI: MISCELLANEOUS
Section 17. Establishment of the Digital Markets and Data Unit (DMDU)
17.1. The CCI shall, as soon as may be after the commencement of this Act, establish a specialized unit to be called the Digital Markets and Data Unit (DMDU). 17.2. The DMDU shall be staffed by experts in data science, digital economics, behavioral science, computer engineering, and law, who shall assist the CCI in the effective enforcement of this Act, including market monitoring, inquiry initiation, and technical auditing.
Section 18. Exemption and Review
18.1. The CCI may, upon a reasoned application by an SSDE, grant an exemption from a specific obligation under Chapter III, if the SSDE demonstrates that the obligation would: 18.1.1. Endanger the security or integrity of the Core Digital Service; OR 18.1.2. Compromise the health, safety, or privacy of End Users in India. 18.2. The exemption shall be granted for a fixed period not exceeding one year and shall be subject to periodic review.
Section 19. Power to Make Rules
19.1. The Central Government may, by notification, make rules for carrying out the purposes of this Act. 19.2. Without prejudice to the generality of the foregoing power, such rules may provide for: 19.2.1. The form and manner in which information shall be furnished to the CCI. 19.2.2. The conditions and procedure for compounding offenses under this Act.
Section 20. Power to Make Regulations
20.1. The CCI shall have the power to make regulations, consistent with the provisions of this Act and the rules made thereunder, to specify: 20.1.1. The detailed procedure for the designation, review, and renewal of SSDE status. 20.1.2. The technical and operational standards for compliance with the mandatory obligations under Chapter III. 20.1.3. The format and frequency of the SSDE Compliance Report.
Section 21. Appeal to the Appellate Tribunal
21.1. Any SSDE or person aggrieved by a final order or direction of the CCI under this Act may prefer an appeal to the Appellate Tribunal established under the Competition Act, 2002, within a period of sixty days from the date on which a copy of the order or direction is received.
Section 22. Protection of Action Taken in Good Faith
22.1. No suit, prosecution, or other legal proceeding shall lie against the Central Government, the CCI, any member of the CCI, the DMDU, or any officer of the CCI, for anything which is in good faith done or intended to be done in pursuance of this Act or any rules or regulations made thereunder.
